## **What are Awards?**

Awards are contracts that have already been awarded to a company. The procurement cycle is complete and the government has issued a contract. This data is sourced from [**USAspending.gov**](http://USAspending.gov). Use the **Awards module** for competitive intelligence, re-compete identification, market research, and understanding who is winning work in your space.\
**How to get there: Sidebar → Click "Awards"**

## **Browsing and Filtering Awards**

- Click **"Awards"** in the sidebar. The awards table loads with all indexed awarded contracts.
- Use the **filter controls** at the top of the table to narrow results by **Agency**, **NAICS Code**, date range, or other criteria.
- After selecting your filters, the table updates immediately to show only matching results.
- To clear filters, click the **X** on each filter tag or use the **reset button**.

## **Viewing Award Details**

- In the awards table, click on any award row.
- The award detail page opens on a single scrollable view showing **Award ID**, **Agency**, **NAICS Code**, **Dates** (award date, start date, end date, expiration date), **Contract Value**, and **Recipient** (the company that won).
- The **agency name** and **recipient name** are clickable links. Clicking the recipient takes you to that organization's **Entity profile**.

## **Favoriting (Bookmarking) Awards**

- While browsing the awards table, locate the award you want to save.
- Click the **bookmark/favorite icon** on that award's row. The icon changes to indicate the award has been saved.
- To access your saved awards later, navigate to the **Favorite Contracts page** within the Awards section.
- On the Favorite Contracts page, all your bookmarked awards are listed. Click any of them to jump to the full detail page.

## **Expiring Contracts**

The **Expiring Contracts page** shows awards that are approaching their contract expiration date for the current month. These are **re-compete opportunities**. When a contract expires, the government typically needs to re-solicit the work.\
**How to get there:** Within the Awards section → **Expiring Contracts**

- Navigate to **Expiring Contracts** within the Awards section.
- The page displays all awards expiring in the current month.
- Use the **filter controls** to narrow results by agency, NAICS code, or contract type.
- Click on any **expiring contract** to view its full award detail page.

## **Award Analytics**

**Analytics** gives you a visual summary of award activity for a selected fiscal year.\
**How to get there:** Within the Awards section → **Analytics**

- Navigate to **Analytics** within the Awards section.
- The page defaults to the most recent fiscal year with data. Use the **fiscal year selector** at the top to switch between years (e.g., 2024, 2025, 2026).
- Review the summary metrics at the top: **Total Awards Count**, **Total Value**, and **Unique Recipients**.
- Scroll down to view the **Top NAICS Codes chart** showing which industry sectors received the most awards.
- Below that, the **Set-Aside Distribution chart** shows the proportion of awards by set-aside type: **No Set-Aside**, **Small Business Set-Aside**, **8(a)**, **HUBZone**, and others.
- The **Top Awards by Value table** lists the highest-value contracts. Click the **sort toggle** to switch between ascending and descending order.
- Every **award** in the table is clickable. Click it to jump to the award detail page.
- Every **entity (company) name** is also clickable. Click it to view that organization's **Entity profile**.

## **Entities (Organizations)**

Entities are the companies and organizations that receive federal awards. Every award is linked to the **entity** that won it.

### **Viewing an Entity Profile**

- From any award detail page, click the **recipient (entity) name link**.
- The entity profile opens showing: **total number of awards** this organization has received, **total combined dollar value** of all their awards.
- Below that, the **Recent Awards section** lists the entity's most recent contract awards in chronological order. Each award is clickable.
- Scroll further to see **graphs and charts** showing the entity's contracting activity over time.
- At the bottom, view the **Top Agencies** section (which agencies award the most to this entity) and **Top Recipients** section for competitive context.

### **Searching for Entities**

- Navigate to the **Entity Search page** within the Awards section.
- Type a **company or organization name** into the search bar.
- Results update as you type. Click any entity name to open their **full profile**.

## **AI Chat for Awards**

The AI Chat lets you ask questions about the **awards database** in plain English instead of filtering tables manually.\
**How to get there:** Within the Awards section → **AI Chat**

- Open the **AI Chat** from within the Awards section.
- Type a question in the text input. For example: "What is the highest-valued award in the database?"
- Press **Enter** or click **Send**. The AI queries the database and responds with the answer.
- You can ask **follow-up questions** in the same conversation. The AI keeps track of the context.

**Examples of questions you can ask:**\
— "Show me all awards for **Adept Engineering Solutions**."\
— "What are the most recent awards from the **Department of Defense**?"\
— "Which companies received the most awards under **NAICS code 541511**?"\
— "What is the total value of all awards in **fiscal year 2024**?"\
— "Give me the **highest-valued award** and tell me which agency issued it."

## **Projects (Scoped Research Environments)**

Projects let you create **focused research workspaces** with a specific instruction and optional file uploads. Every chat you create inside a project is scoped to that instruction. The AI only answers within the context you defined.\
**How to get there:** Within the Awards section → **Projects**

### **Creating a Project**

- Navigate to **Projects** within the Awards section.
- Click **"Create New Project"**.
- Enter a **Project Name**. Make it descriptive. For example, "Department of Agriculture IT Awards."
- Write an **Instruction**. This tells the AI what to focus on. For example, "Only focus on the Department of Agriculture" or "Analyze IT service contracts above \$1M."
- Optionally, **upload supporting files**. Supported formats: **PDF, CSV, Excel (XLSX/XLS), Word (DOC/DOCX), TXT**. These files become part of the AI's context for all chats in this project.
- Click **Save**.

[INSERT IMAGE: The project creation form showing the Name field, Instruction text area, and file upload area. Click Create New Project and capture the form.]

### **Using a Project**

- Open your saved project from the **Projects list**.
- Click **"New Chat"** to start a conversation thread within this project.
- Type any question. The AI will answer based only on the instruction you set and the files you uploaded. For example, if your instruction says "Only focus on the Department of Agriculture" and you ask "What awards are available?" the AI returns only Department of Agriculture results.
- You can create multiple chat threads inside the same project. Each thread shares the same instruction and file context but has its own **conversation history**.

## **Research (Scheduled AI Queries)**

Research lets you set up an **AI question** to run automatically on a **recurring schedule**. Each run's results are saved so you can compare them over time.\
**How to get there: Within the Awards section → Research**

### **Scheduling a Research Query**

- In the AI Chat within Awards, type the question you want to run on a recurring schedule. For example: "What are the latest awards for cloud computing services?"
- Click the **"Schedule Research"** button.
- Choose a frequency: **Daily, Weekly, Monthly, or Yearly**.
- Enter a descriptive name. For example, "**Cloud Awards Monthly Report**."
- Click **Save**. The AI will now run this question automatically at your chosen interval.

### **Viewing Research Results**

- Navigate to the **Research page** within Awards.
- You will see a list of all your **scheduled research items** with their names and frequencies.
- Click on any research item to expand it.
- Inside, you will see results organized by **execution date**. For example, entries for December 11, December 16, December 27. Each shows the AI's response for that date.
- Compare results across dates to spot **trends or changes**.
- Click the **download button** to export results as **Word, Excel, or CSV**.
