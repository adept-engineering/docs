**The Pipeline** tracks opportunities through a structured 8-stage business development lifecycle. It mirrors the standard federal government capture management process. The Pipeline connects forecasts, opportunities, and awards into a single view with charts and links at every stage.

**How to get there:** Sidebar → Click **Pipeline** → This expands to show **Dashboard** and **Funnel**

## The 8 pipeline stages

| Stage | Name | What happens |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Market Identification** | Identifying relevant markets, agencies, and procurement trends. Deciding which sectors to pursue. |
| 2 | **Long-Term Positioning** | Building agency relationships, attending industry days, and positioning your company as a known entity. |
| 3 | **Opportunity ID & Qualification** | Identified a specific opportunity and making the bid or no-bid decision. |
| 4 | **Capture Planning** | Developing your win strategy, identifying teaming partners, and understanding how the government will evaluate proposals. |
| 5 | **Proposal Planning** | Outlining the proposal structure, assigning writers, and setting internal deadlines. |
| 6 | **Proposal Development** | Write, review, and refine the proposal content. |
| 7 | **Submission & Negotiation** | Submitted the proposal and handled any government clarification requests or negotiations. |
| 8 | **Contract Award & Management** | Win the contract and manage execution and performance. |

## Using the Pipeline Dashboard

**How to get there:** Pipeline → Click **Dashboard**

1. Click **Dashboard** under Pipeline in the sidebar.
2. The top of the page shows four metric cards:

| Metric | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **Total Records** | Total opportunities in your pipeline across all stages. |
| **Active** | Opportunities currently being pursued (not yet won or lost). |
| **Won** | Opportunities that resulted in a contract award. |
| **Win Rate** | Percentage of pipeline opportunities that resulted in a win. |

3. Below the metrics, a funnel chart shows how many opportunities sit at each of the 8 stages along with the combined dollar value per stage.
4. Use this view to identify bottlenecks. For example, if many opportunities are at Capture Planning but few are progressing to Proposal Development, that stage needs attention.

## Managing pipeline records

**How to get there:** Pipeline → Click **Funnel**

1. Click **Funnel** under Pipeline in the sidebar. This shows a table of all your pipeline records.
2. The table has columns: Title, Agency, Stage, and Actions.

| Action | How to do it |
| --- | --- |
| **Add a record** | Click the **Create** button, or add an opportunity from any opportunity detail page in the Opportunities section. |
| **Move to a different stage** | Click the **Actions** menu on a record's row and select the target stage. |
| **Edit a record** | Click **Actions** → **Edit**. |
| **Delete a record** | Click **Actions** → **Delete**. |
