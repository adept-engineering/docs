## **What are Forecasts?**

Forecasts are **future procurement opportunities** that agencies have planned and announced but have not yet posted as formal solicitations on [**SAM.gov**](http://SAM.gov). Most federal agencies publish their planned procurements for the upcoming fiscal year in centralized **forecast databases**. Kontratar scrapes this data and makes it available to you.

**Important distinction:** **Awards** are contracts already awarded. **Forecasts** are planned future procurements not yet solicited. **Opportunities** are active solicitations currently on [**SAM.gov**](http://SAM.gov) or **eBuy**. The typical lifecycle is: **Forecast → Opportunity → Award**.

You cannot **bid on, apply for, or respond to a forecast**. Forecasts are **informational only** — they tell you what is coming so you can prepare before the competition. You can, however, use the **Campaigns feature (Section 6)** to proactively reach out to contracting officers about forecasted opportunities.

**How to get there:** Sidebar → Click **"Forecasts"**

## **Browsing Forecasts**

- Click **"Forecasts"** in the sidebar. The forecast table loads.
- Each row shows: **Agency** (who is planning the procurement), **Description** (what they plan to buy), **Estimated Value** (the expected contract value range, such as \$20M–\$50M), **Anticipated Quarter** (when they expect to release the solicitation, such as Q4 FY2025), and **Status** (Active or Archive).
- If the **Status** column says "Archive," the anticipated release date has already passed. That means the forecast has likely turned into an opportunity on [**SAM.gov**](http://SAM.gov) or has already been awarded.
- Click on any forecast row to open the **detail page** with **agency contacts, NAICS codes, timeline, and any additional notes**.

## **Forecast Detail Page**

- From the forecast table, click any row.
- The **detail page** opens with the full information: **agency, description, estimated value, anticipated quarter, NAICS code**, and any **agency-published notes or contacts**.
- Review this information to decide whether this **future opportunity** is worth pursuing.

## **Calendar View**

- On the Forecasts page, switch to the **Calendar view** using the **view toggle**.
- Navigate to any **month or date range** using the calendar controls.
- The calendar shows which forecasts are expected to be released in each time period.
- Click on any **date block** to see the forecasts scheduled for that period.

## **AI Chat for Forecasts**

Works the same as **Awards AI Chat**, but queries **forecast data**.

- Open the **AI Chat** within the Forecasts section.
- Type a question about **upcoming procurements**. Examples:\
  — "What are the upcoming forecast opportunities for **EDI systems**?"\
  — "Show me **Department of State** procurements expected in Q4."\
  — "Which agencies are planning to award contracts over **\$10M** in the next year?"\
  — "List forecast opportunities related to **cloud computing services**."
- Press **Enter** or click **Send**. The AI queries the **forecast database** and returns results.

## **Research for Forecasts**

Works exactly like **Awards Research**. You can **schedule recurring queries** (daily, weekly, monthly, yearly) to automatically track specific **forecast topics**. Results are saved by date and can be **compared or downloaded** as **Word, Excel, or CSV**.

## **Projects for Forecasts**

Works exactly like **Awards Projects**. Create a **scoped environment** with an **instruction** (e.g., "Only focus on Department of Housing forecasts") and optional **file uploads**. All chats within the project stay within that context.
