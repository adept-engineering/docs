**What is eBuy?**

eBuy is the GSA's online procurement system where government buyers post Requests for Quote (RFQ), Requests for Proposal (RFP), and Requests for Information (RFI) exclusively to companies that hold GSA Schedules. With Version 2, Kontratar connects directly to your eBuy portal account and pulls these opportunities into the platform so you can view them alongside your [SAM.gov](http://SAM.gov) opportunities.

eBuy opportunities are tied to the NAICS codes (referred to as SIN categories on the eBuy portal) that you are already subscribed to on the eBuy platform itself. You do not need to set up any additional subscriptions inside Kontratar. As long as you are subscribed to the relevant SIN categories on the eBuy portal, Kontratar will automatically surface those opportunities.

Kontratar generates AI-powered proposals for eBuy opportunities using the same proposal engine as [SAM.gov](http://SAM.gov) opportunities. The proposal quality and structure are identical regardless of source.

**How to get there:** Sidebar → Opportunities → Click "eBuy

## **Browsing eBuy Opportunities**

### **Steps**

1. Click **"eBuy"** under Opportunities in the sidebar. The eBuy opportunities table loads.
2. The table displays five columns: Title (the opportunity name), NAICS Code (the SIN/NAICS code), Buyer (the government agency posting the request), Notice ID (the unique eBuy identifier), and Date (the posting date).
3. Look at the Notice ID prefix to identify the type. RFP means Request for Proposal. RFQ means Request for Quote. RFI means Request for Information.
4. Click on any row to open the full opportunity detail page.

## **Viewing eBuy Opportunity Details**

### **Steps**

 1. From the eBuy table, click on any opportunity row.
 2. The detail page opens with multiple tabs across the top: Details, Description, Keywords, Partners Ranking, Documents, Requirements, Similar Opportunities, Proposal Outline, and Submission Requirements.
 3. Click **"Details"** to see the opportunity metadata — title, NAICS code, buyer, notice ID, dates, and point of contact.
 4. Click **"Description"** for the full solicitation text.
 5. Click **"Keywords"** for AI-extracted relevant terms.
 6. Click **"Documents"** for any attached solicitation files.
 7. Click **"Requirements"** for extracted compliance items.
 8. Click **"Proposal Outline"** for the AI-generated proposal structure tailored to this opportunity.
 9. Click **"Similar Opportunities"** to see AI-identified opportunities that match this one.
10. Click **"Submission Requirements"** for deadlines and submission instructions.
11. To go back to the eBuy table, click the back arrow or use the sidebar navigation.
